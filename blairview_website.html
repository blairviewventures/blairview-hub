The Diagnostic

Every team has a theory.
Almost none of them are right.

The experience gap — the distance between what a team believes they shipped and what a user actually encounters — is the most expensive problem in consumer product. It compounds quietly, in churn, in NPS, in growth that stalls without a clear cause.

That gap is diagnosable. Not with a framework applied generically — with specific, evidence-based findings about what is actually happening in your product.

  • 01Retention metrics that don't respond to product changes
  • 02A launch that underperformed and produced no consensus on why
  • 03A second product surface quietly eroding trust in the first
  • 04An AI feature with adoption numbers that don't match internal confidence
  • 05A product-market fit narrative that diverges from what users actually do

The moment users cross from useful to indispensable is specific, fragile, and almost always invisible to the team that built it.

The Practice

17 years. Any industry. The credential is scale of consumer impact.

The skills that made HBO GO a product people loved before streaming had a roadmap are the same skills that close a retention gap at a Series B company. The vertical changes. The consumer experience problem doesn't.

Financial services is one vertical this practice has crossed. Consumer-facing product excellence is the discipline — and it transfers to any company building something real users need to love.
