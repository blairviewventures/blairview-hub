The Practice

Seventeen years building products that had to earn trust from people who didn't care how hard they were to build.

The vertical changes. The consumer experience problem doesn't. What made HBO GO a product people loved before streaming had a roadmap is the same discipline that closes a retention gap at a Series B company. Pattern recognition earned from launching at scale when no one had a playbook for it.

Most of the work happens after launch day. Getting a product out the door requires a different kind of judgment than knowing what to do once users are actually in it. What to kill. What to keep. What earns the next build cycle.

Consumer-facing product excellence is the discipline. It transfers to any company building something real users need to love.

How to Know if There's a Fit

Every team has a theory.
This practice makes sense of what to do with it.

The experience gap is the distance between what a team believes they shipped and what a user actually encounters. It is the most expensive problem in consumer product. It compounds quietly, in the moments users disengage, in the trust that erodes before anyone has a name for it, in growth that stalls without a clear cause.

That gap is diagnosable. With a specific read on what's actually broken and where.

  • 01A launch that underperformed and produced no consensus on why
  • 02A second product surface quietly eroding trust in the first
  • 03An AI feature with strong internal conviction and adoption that doesn't match it
  • 04A consumer product launch inside 90 days with no outside read on what users will actually encounter
  • 05A product that hit its launch metrics with no clear directive on how to take the next step in its evolution
