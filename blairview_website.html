The diagnostic

Every team has a theory about why the product isn't converting. Almost none of them are right.

The experience gap — the distance between what a team believes they shipped and what a user actually encounters — is the most expensive problem in consumer product. It compounds quietly, shows up in churn and NPS and stalled growth, and resists every fix that doesn't start with an accurate diagnosis.

That diagnosis is the practice. Not frameworks applied generically. A specific, evidence-based finding about what is actually happening in your product — and a prioritized path to fixing it.

  • Retention metrics that don't respond to product changes
  • A launch that underperformed and produced no consensus on why
  • A second product surface that's eroding trust in the first
  • An AI feature with adoption numbers that don't match internal confidence
  • A product-market fit narrative that diverges from user behavior data
